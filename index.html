Who I am

A Collection of My Experiences.

Hi, I'm Rojit, originally from Nepal, currently based in Sydney, Australia as an international student studying Information Technology, and running my own company, Isrario Holdings.

Before tech took over, I spent years as a chef, crafting dishes that told stories. Parallel to that, I roamed the hills, valleys, and festivals of Nepal as a freelance photographer, freezing fleeting moments in time.

These days I'm channeling that same creative energy - precision from the kitchen, patience from behind the lens - into building digital products and businesses from Sydney.

Through the lens

Moments I Kept

A selection of photographs from my time as a freelance photographer in Nepal — from mountain landscapes to street life and cultural moments.

Dolakha, Nepal (3,842m)

Kalinchowk: The Alpine Gateway

A famous Himalayan pilgrimage site and hill station known for its panoramic mountain views and the sacred Bhagwati Temple. Located above the stone-housed Kuri Village, it is a top destination for winter snow, trekking, and sunrise views over the Gauri Shankar and Langtang ranges.

Bhaktapur, Nepal

Betal: The Guardian of Biska Jatra

A close-up study of the Netra Dan ritual, where the eyes of Lord Bhairav are ceremoniously placed before the New Year chariot procession. This moment signifies the spiritual awakening of the deity, bridging the gap between ancient tantric tradition and the living heritage of the Newar community.

Chitwan, Nepal

Tharu Culture Program

Captured through a phone screen in a dark hall -the Tharu cultural dance performance alive in a frame within a frame.

Bhaktapur, Nepal

The Artist's Hand

A close-up of the traditional painting process on the Bhailkha (Bhairav’s chariot). This image captures the skilled hand of a local artist applying vibrant pigments to the massive wooden structure, a sacred task performed annually to restore the chariot's spiritual and visual power before the New Year procession

Kathmandu, Nepal

The Wisdom Eyes

Rising above the chaos of the city, the wisdom eyes of the Swayambhunath Stupa look out toward the four corners of the world. Framed by prayer flags that carry whispers to the sky, this image captures the quiet, eternal watchfulness that has defined the Kathmandu Valley for centuries.

Kathmandu, Nepal

Force on the Streets

Riot police march in formation through a busy Kathmandu street, a city paused, watching, waiting.

Chitwan, Nepal

River Crossing at Dusk

A long wooden canoe carries passengers across a grey river as dusk falls - timeless and serene.

Basantapur, Nepal

Narayan Dai ko Masangalli ko MoMo

A plate of steaming momo on a worn wooden table — Nepal's most beloved comfort food, simple and perfect.

Bhaktapur, Nepal

Biska Jatra: The Great Tug-of-War

Capturing the raw energy and collective strength of the Bhaktapur community. This traditional tug-of-war between the upper and lower parts of the city is a central event of Biska Jatra, symbolizing unity and the powerful start of the New Year.

Bara, Nepal

Fields of Gold: Kalaiya

There is a specific stillness found in the rural outskirts of Kalaiya — a mix of humid air, the scent of parched earth, and the steady movement of a lone worker. This shot documents the raw, unpolished beauty of life in southern Nepal, far from the festival lights and city noise.
